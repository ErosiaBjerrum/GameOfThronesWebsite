House Lannister

"When the lion stirs, kingdoms tremble. For gold may buy swords, but it is fear that keeps them drawn — and House Lannister has mastered both."

— Petyr Baelish

In the golden west of the Seven Kingdoms stands House Lannister, Lords of Casterly Rock, Wardens of the West, and masters of the richest veins of gold ever drawn from the bones of the earth.

From their mighty seat, hewn into the living stone above the Sunset Sea, they have watched the tides of history rise and break against the shores of Westeros, unbowed and unbroken.

Their banner bears a lion rampant of shining gold upon a field of crimson — a sigil not merely stitched in silk, but etched into the very soul of the realm. For where the lion walks, men take heed; where it roars, kingdoms tremble.

Ancient in lineage and unyielding in pride, the Lannisters trace their dominion back to the Age of Heroes, when cunning and boldness won them Casterly Rock itself. Since that dawn, their name has been spoken with equal parts reverence and dread.

Gold is their lifeblood, yet power is their true inheritance. With coffers deeper than many kingdoms and alliances forged in both marriage and war, House Lannister has long bent the currents of court and crown to its will.

Kings have risen through their favor. Kings have fallen by their design.

In council chambers draped in red and gilded splendor, their words are measured like coin — precise, deliberate, and seldom wasted. In the field of battle, their hosts shine like a river of flame, disciplined and relentless beneath banners of scarlet and gold.

The West does not forget. The West does not forgive.

Though their official words proclaim, “Hear Me Roar!”, the realm knows another truth whispered from tavern to throne room: A Lannister always pays his debts.

And whether that payment comes in gold, in blood, or in fire — it always comes due.
